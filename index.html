What is CCH?

After three years (possibly more!) of hiatus, we are proud to announce that we are hosting the first schoolwide coding competition at Monta Vista High School! The Cross Club Hackathon is organized by the three major tech clubs on campus: the Computer Science club, the Data Science club, and the Artificial Intelligence club. We aim to provide an opportunity to MVHS students to show off their talents in programming and create projects that have a real impact on the world around us!

When will CCH 2023 take place?

CCH 2023 starts on Saturday, March 11th, 2023 and ends on Saturday, March 25th, 2023. We plan on holding a virtual awards ceremony on Saturday, April 4th, 2023. Exact details will soon be announced on this website!

Let’s talk logistics!

CCH is a team-based competition. Participants will compete in teams of 4, and if you don’t have a team, we will connect you with other participants who are also looking for teammates.

CCH also had two divisions: Challenger and Hardcore. The Challenger division is for members who are taking or have taken Java or have a similar level of experience in coding. The Hardcore division is for members who are taking or have taken AP CS A or have a similar level of experience in coding. This way, everyone gets to compete against people at their level, and Challengers also get an opportunity to win prizes. When you create a team, please ensure that all of your members are Challengers or all of your members are Hardcore participants; do not mix and match. Officers reserve the right to modify teams to ensure fairness across both divisions.

Over the two weeks of the tournament, you and your team are tasked with creating a project that will blow us away! In order to be a valid submission, your project must satisfy the following three criteria:

  • The project must relate to the theme of the hackathon (see below).
  • The project must be an application, a website, or a similar format.
    • Just training a dataset with AI does not count as a valid project. It must be implemented into one of the above formats.
    • Console-based projects are acceptable, but you may be at a disadvantage.
  • The project must incorporate Artificial Intelligence or Data Science in some way.
    • For competitors who do not have experience in AI or Data Science, the MV Data Science Club is generously offering a Data Science workshop for beginners. The workshop will take place on Thursday, March 16th, 2023, at lunch in E204. (This is the same time and place as the MVCS meeting.)

We also request that people do not submit “mocks,” or front-ends that don’t actually have any functionality.

What’s the theme for CCH 2023?

The theme for the competition will be released on this website on Saturday, March 11th, 2023, at 3:00 PM. All projects must be related to this theme, but you will still have plenty of room for creativity!

What’s the judging criteria?

Projects will be judged by creativity, applicability, and potential for impact.

Although we will not be judging your code, we will request that you submit your code so our judges can run the application and ensure that your project is not a mock.

You will also be asked to create a short video to present your project. There is no minimum or maximum time limit for this video, but make a reasonable judgment.

Are there prizes?

Of course, there will be prizes! (Yay!) The first place team in both the Challenger division and the Hardcore Division will receive prizes. However, what exactly are the prizes? That’s a mystery… you’ll have to participate to find out!

How do I participate?

Registration forms are here! If you already have a team of four, fill out this form. Only one team member needs to fill it out. This form is due by the end of the day on Saturday, March 18th, 2023.

On the other hand, if you're still looking for more teammates to complete your team, fill out this form instead. We will connect you with other participants looking for teammates. This form is due by the end of the day on Friday, March 17th, 2023.

How do I submit?

Deta.ils are tentative, but we plan on holding submissions through Devpost. Submission details will be released here on Sunday, March 19th, 2023, after registration has closed.
